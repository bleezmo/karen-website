A Little About Me

Read here about some stuff I've done.

I am currently a member of The Dramatists Guild Of America,Inc. Also, I interned with them in their creative affairs department. I started as a beauty writer (yes, you read that right.) I authored a beauty book called The Lipstick Classified. I've received an A.A. Degree in Theatre Arts, Performance Concentration as well as being awarded the Theatre Arts Award and graduating with honors. Some highlights so far have been They Call Me A Hero be performed as a staged reading at the Mac Playhouse as well as Surprise Party being read at The Dramatists Guild. Also, I have had multiple rolls in Angels In America 1&2 (Woman from the Bronx/Angel/Mormon Mother) where select theatre students were chosen to act along with professional actors.

I am currently studying at Hunter College working towards my B.A. in Theatre (done May 2014) I'm on the Dean's List and was a finalist for a Presidential Fellowship. I was awarded the Roberts Fellowship and the Lo Bianco Grant. Additionally, I have been in other productions as an actor and have a background as a trained dancer.

Please check back for updates about works in progress and other career news. Blah, blah, blah.

Plays (cont.)

One Act: They Call Me A Hero (2M, 1F) (Drama) Jimmy is a 21 year old who just got shipped back home from serving two tours in the Vietnam War. He is shipped back earlier than expected because he is being awarded the rare Medal of Honor. When he returns back, his surprise visit to Martha, his high school sweetheart, turns into something he didn’t expect when Jimmy shows up unexpectedly at her door and she is unprepared. She is now in college and has become a radical anti-war protestor with her new boyfriend Logan. She confronts Jimmy about the war in an radical way. Just then, Logan shows up and walks into the middle of Martha and Jimmy’s confrontation. (10 Min.)

Plays (cont.)

One Act: Oedipus Rex Satyr Play (2M, 1F, Chorus multiple/flex) (Satirical comedy) A traditional satyr play written from the Greek tragedy Oedipus Rex using rhyming couplets. (approx. 5 min.)


One Act: Surprise Party (4M, 4F) (Dark comedy) A group of friends gather at a brothers and sisters house for a birthday party to find they are hiding a secret. When it is revealed so are secrets about the other guests. (approx. 60 min)

Plays (cont.)

One Act: Step Right Up (2F) (Drama) A chance doctor’s appointment brings a connection between the elderly woman patient who was a sideshow performer and her doctor. (approx. 10 min.)


Scene: Help Wanted $8 An Hour (1M, 1F) (Comedy) A computer programmer puts an add to find someone help him get work done and a college student comes in for the interview. (approx. 7 min)

Plays (cont.)

One Act: Guacamole Vampires Of The Apocalypse (2M,1F) (Comedy) Ralph believes the end of the world is tomorrow because of the Mayan Calendar. He finds $500 in a wallet on the street and asks his friend who sells coffins and his stoner roommate to help him with his plans for the final day of earth. They want the money he found for their own needs. How will they all compromise on what they all need? (approx. 15 min.)

Plays (cont.)

One Act: Women's Room (2F) (Drama) When Becky comes out of the bathroom stall of a women’s room crying because her blind date left in the middle of their date on Valentines Day, she meets Jenelle by the sink mirror beside her. As Jenelle fixes her neck scarf, Becky notices an Adam’s apple and Jenelle tells Becky that she is in the final stages of her transitioning to a woman. They argue about what a real woman is. Then Jenelle confronts Becky on the bullying she did when they were kids. Becky justifies her behavior as tensions rise. Does Becky make the realization about herself that Jenelle hoped she would? (approx. 20 min.)
