A Little About Me

This is the part where you read about my hopes, dreams and inspirations. Where I first "found my voice." Really? If you really want to know that, email me. But, otherwise read here about some stuff I've done.


I've been writing plays for 3 years now and I am currently a member of The Dramatists Guild Of America. I started as a beauty writer (yes, you read that right.) I've received an A.A. Degree in Theatre Arts, Performance Concentration as well as being awarded the Theatre Arts Award as well as graduating with honors. Some highlights so far have been having They Call Me A Hero (a one-act play I wrote) be performed as a staged reading. Also, I have have had multiple rolls in Angels In America 1&2 (Woman from the Bronx/Angel/Mormon Mother) where select theatre students were chosen to act along with professional actors.


I am currently studying at Hunter College working towards my B.A. in Theatre and Philosophy (Almost done.) I'm on the Dean's List. Additionally, I have been in other productions as an actor and have a background as a trained dancer.


Please check back for updates about works in progress and other career news. Blah, blah, blah.

Plays (cont.)

One Act: They Call Me A Hero (1M, 1F) (Drama) Jimmy is a 21 year old who just got shipped back home from serving two tours in the Vietnam War. He is shipped back earlier than expected because he is being awarded the rare Medal of Honor. He has been writing his high school sweetheart Martha, 19, the whole time in the war. When he returns back, his surprise visit to her turns into something he didn’t expect when Jimmy shows up unexpectedly at her door and she is unprepared. (10 Min.)

Plays (cont.)

One Act: Oedipus Rex Satyr Play (2M, 1F, Chorus multiple/flex) (Satirical comedy) A traditional satyr play written from the Greek tragedy Oedipus Rex using rhyming couplets. (approx. 5 min.)


One Act: My Sisters Birthday (4M, 4F) (Dark comedy) A group of friends gather at a brothers and sisters house for a birthday party to find they are hiding a secret. When it is revealed so are secrets about the other guests. (approx. 40 min)

Plays (cont.)

One Act: Step Right Up (2F) (Drama) A chance doctor’s appointment brings a connection between the elderly woman patient who was a sideshow performer and her doctor. (approx. 10 min.)


Scene: Help Wanted $8 An Hour (1M, 1F) (Comedy) A computer programmer puts an add to find someone help him get work done and a college student comes in for the interview. (approx. 7 min)

Plays (cont.)

One Act: Guacamole Vampires Of The Apocalypse (2M,1F) (Comedy) Ralph believes the end of the world is tomorrow because of the Mayan Calendar. He finds $500 in a wallet on the street and asks his friend who sells coffins and his stoner roommate to help him with his plans for the final day of earth. They want the money he found for their own needs. How will they all compromise on what they all need? (approx. 15 min.)
